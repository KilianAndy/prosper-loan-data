Investigation Overview¶

My goal is to investigate about the borrowers alpha prosper ratings and where the majority of those borrowers lay. Get to determine the stated monthly income of the borrowers and how the increase in the number of loans original amount affected the number of investors. I also want to know how the verification of the borrowers affects the number of investors in a particular listing. I'm also interested in knowing how the number of investors are affected by the borrowers original loan amount and how the two relate with the monthly loan payments.

Here are the key insights in my slide show:

  1. Most of the borrowers had an alpha prosper rating of C while the least had an alpha prosper rating of AA. Majority of the borrowers lay between the ratings of A, B, C and D.
  2. Majority of the borrowers had stated their stated monthly income as about 4000 to 4500 US dollars. There were about 91 borrowers who stated their monthly income above 50000 US dollars while only 2 borrowers states their monthly income above 500000 US dollars and the highest borrower stating a monthly income of around 1,750,000 US dollars.
  3. An increase in the number of the loans original amount seemed to have increased the number of investors lending borrowers loans.
  4. Most investors lent their money to borrowers who had their source(s) of income verified throughout the dataset. Also, for borrowers who could do a monthly loan payment of more than $1750 all had their source(s) of income verified.
  5. The number of investors in the state with the most number of borrowers increases with an increase in the number of loan original amount. The amount of monthly loan payments seems to have been increasing with an increase in the loan original amount as well as an increase in the number of investors.

The dataset contains 113937 rows and 81 columns most of which have numerical type data. Other columns have the categorical type data while others have a date-time type data.

The unique column being that of listing keys and so I'm going to drop duplicates if there are any.

(Visualization 4)¶

600 or more investors lent money to borrowers who had a verifiable source(s) of income. Only one borrower who made a monthly payment of above 1500 had an unverified source(s) of income.

Majority of borrowers who did not have a verifiable source(s) of income had less than 400 investors lending them money. Of those investors, a majority of them made less than $\$600$ monthly loan payments.

For every level of monthly loan payments, those who had a verifiable source(s) of income had the most number of investors lending them money compared to those with unverified source(s) of income.

All borrowers who made more than $\$810$ and had only one investor lending them money had their source(s) of income verified.

The number of investors in the state with the most number of borrowers increases with an increase in the number of loan original amount.
